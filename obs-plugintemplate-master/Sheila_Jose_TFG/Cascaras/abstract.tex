\chapter*{Abstract}

\section*{\tituloPortadaEngVal}

In recent years, it has become increasingly common for television programs to incorporate augmented reality (AR) effects during live presentations. This technology enables the integration of virtual elements—such as three-dimensional graphics, interactive visualizations, or real-time simulations—that appear to coexist physically within the studio environment and interact naturally with the presenters. By doing so, AR enhances the visual narrative, facilitates the understanding of complex information, and increases the overall aesthetic and communicative impact of the broadcast.

This Final Degree Project aims to explore the potential of augmented reality technology in the context of live streaming, specifically through platforms such as YouTube, using OBS Studio as the main production tool. The study investigates how AR can be integrated into streaming workflows to replicate, in a more accessible and cost-effective way, the advanced visual effects typically found in professional television environments. Particular emphasis is placed on the application of these techniques to programming contest broadcasts, a format where visual clarity, contextual data representation, and audience engagement are essential. Implementing AR elements in this setting has the potential to significantly enhance the viewer’s experience, improve content comprehension, and add a layer of visual professionalism to the production.
