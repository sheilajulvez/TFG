\chapter*{Conclusions and Future Work}
\label{cap:conclusions}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Conclusions and Future Work}

Although the plugin has demonstrated significant functionality, there remain numerous opportunities for improvement and expansion that could further enhance its usefulness and scope. Up to this point, the plugin has been tested in controlled environments and has not yet been applied in an actual live broadcast, such as a programming contest or other interactive event. In such contexts, the system could provide real-time information that makes the experience more complete and engaging.

For example, it could display countdown clocks for the contest, graphs showing participants’ points or progress, or leaderboards that update automatically based on results. Incorporating external data, either through online service APIs or local log files, would allow for more dynamic and personalized experiences, making the broadcast not only visually appealing but also informative and relevant for viewers.

Beyond external data integration, another promising direction is the implementation of event-driven animations in real time. For instance, a 3D model could change its position, color, or initiate a specific movement when certain conditions are met, such as the completion of a task or the appearance of new relevant data. This type of interactivity would enhance viewer immersion, giving the impression that virtual elements respond directly to the events of the broadcast.

Additionally, there is the potential to incorporate more advanced tracking techniques, such as object or space recognition without relying on physical markers. This would allow 3D models to adapt to more varied environments, removing dependence on QR or ArUco codes, and would enable use in more dynamic scenarios with moving cameras or changing spaces. Consequently, the plugin could be applied in more professional broadcasts, where flexibility and robustness are critical.

Finally, direct viewer interaction represents a highly promising avenue. Through web interfaces, mobile applications, or even camera-captured gestures, users could modify the position, rotation, size, or visibility of models during the broadcast. This would not only increase audience engagement but could also enable real-time collaborative experiences, where multiple viewers interact with the same virtual elements, transforming the broadcast into a more dynamic and participatory environment.

In conclusion, this project establishes a solid foundation for integrating augmented reality into OBS Studio, demonstrating its potential to enrich live content presentation. At the same time, it opens a clear path for future developments that incorporate external data, greater interactivity, event-driven animations, advanced tracking, and multiple complex scenarios, bringing AR closer to professional, creative, and educational contexts where real-time broadcasts can deliver meaningful, engaging, and highly customizable experiences.
